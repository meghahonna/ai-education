🧑‍🔬 Try It: Watch Context Rot in Real Time

Want to see how quickly a model’s attention degrades? Follow these steps to simulate a bloated prompt and watch the risk meter climb.

1
Select “Donor Data Analysis” from the Sample Prompt dropdown above. Notice the rot meter shows Low Risk.
2
Click “Copy Text” below to grab the sample conversation block, then paste it into the Conversation History box (section 6). Paste it 10 times.
3
Watch the rot meter, instruction retention, and example recall cards update in real time as the context fills up.
4
Now switch the model from Claude Sonnet 4 (200K) to Mistral Medium (32K) — same prompt, dramatically different risk.
5
Try trimming the history back down. Notice how the risk drops. This is why concise context engineering matters.
User: Can you analyze the Q3 donor retention numbers? I’m seeing a dip in recurring gifts and want to understand if it’s seasonal or if we’re losing donors permanently. Also, the board is asking for a year-over-year comparison. Assistant: Looking at the Q3 data, I can see a 12% decline in recurring gift retention compared to Q2. Your “Loyal Recurring” donors (monthly gifts for 12+ months) are stable at 94% retention. The dip is concentrated in newer recurring donors (under 6 months) where retention dropped from 71% to 58%. User: That’s helpful. Can you also pull the lapsed donor numbers? And cross-reference with the golf tournament attendees? I want to see if event donors are converting to recurring. Assistant: Of the 340 golf tournament donors from 2024, only 23 (6.8%) converted to recurring gifts. However, their average one-time gift was $475, well above your $180 average. The gap suggests a stewardship opportunity rather than disinterest.

💡 Tip: After pasting 10 times, the Conversation History section alone will consume thousands of tokens. This simulates what happens in a real multi-turn chat where context accumulates and early instructions get pushed out of the model’s attention window.
